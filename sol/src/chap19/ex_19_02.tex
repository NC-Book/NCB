\exercise{Observability and controllability}{2}
For the Jacobian from the previous question compute the observability and controllability Gramians
\eqn{
{\bf P} = - \sum_{m,n} \frac{
(\vec{w_m}\vec{w_n^\dag})  (\vec{v_m^\dag}\vec{v_n}) 
}{\lambda_n+\lambda_m^\dag},\qqq 
{\bf Q} = - \sum_{m,n} \frac{
(\vec{v_m}\vec{v_n^\dag})  (\vec{w_m^\dag}\vec{w_n}) 
}{\lambda_n+\lambda_m^\dag} 
}
and then compute the observability $P_{ii}$ and controlability $Q_{ii}$ of the two nodes. 

\solution 
From the previous exercise we know the eigenvectors 
\eq{
   \vec{v_1}=\avec{1\\ 0},  \qqq \vec{w_1}^\dag = (1,3),  
}
\eq{
   \vec{v_2}=\avec{-3\\ 1},  \qqq \vec{w_2}^\dag = (0,1),
}
and the eigenvalues 
\eq{
   \lambda_1 = -1, \qqq \lambda_2=-2
} 
\eqa{
{\bf P} &=& - \sum_{m,n} \frac{
(\vec{w_m}\vec{w_n^\dag})  (\vec{v_m^\dag}\vec{v_n}) 
}{\lambda_n+\lambda_m^\dag} \\
   &=&  \frac{
(\vec{w_1}\vec{w_1^\dag})  (\vec{v_1^\dag}\vec{v_1}) 
}{\lambda_1+\lambda_1^\dag} +  
\frac{
(\vec{w_1}\vec{w_2^\dag})  (\vec{v_1^\dag}\vec{v_2}) 
}{\lambda_2+\lambda_1^\dag} \\
\frac{
(\vec{w_2}\vec{w_1^\dag})  (\vec{v_2^\dag}\vec{v_1}) 
}{\lambda_1+\lambda_2^\dag} +  
\frac{
(\vec{w_2}\vec{w_2^\dag})  (\vec{v_2^\dag}\vec{v_2}) 
}{\lambda_2+\lambda_2^\dag} \\
}
Let's consider the numerators of these four terms separately:
\eqa{
(\vec{w_1}\vec{w_1^\dag})  (\vec{v_1^\dag}\vec{v_1}) &=& \left(\avec{1\\ 3}(1,3)\right)  \left((1,0)\avec{1\\ 0}\right) \\
   &=& \avecc{1 & 3 \\ 3 & 9} \cdot  1 \\
(\vec{w_1}\vec{w_2^\dag})  (\vec{v_1^\dag}\vec{v_2}) &=& \left(\avec{1 \\ 3}(0,1)\right) \left((1,0)\avec{-3\\ 1}\right) \\  
&=& \avecc{  0 & 1 \\ 0 & 3} \cdot{-3} \\  
(\vec{w_2}\vec{w_1^\dag})  (\vec{v_2^\dag}\vec{v_1}) &=& \left(\avec{0 \\ 1}(1,3)\right) \left((-3,1)\avec{1\\ 0}\right) \\
   &=& \avecc{ 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 3} \cdot (-3) \\
(\vec{w_2}\vec{w_2^\dag})  (\vec{v_2^\dag}\vec{v_2}) &=& \left(\avec{0 \\ 1}(0,1)\right )  \left((-3,1)\avec{-3\\ 1}\right) \\
  &=& \avecc{0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1} \cdot (10)
}
Substituting back yields 
\eqa{
{\bf P} &=& \frac{1}{2} \avecc{1 & 3 \\ 3 & 9} - \avecc{  0 & 1 \\ 0 & 3} - \avecc{ 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 3} + \frac{5}{2} \avecc{0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1} \\
  &=& \frac{1}{2} \avecc{1 & 1\\ 1 & 2}
}
So the observability of the first node is $1/2$ whereas the observability of the second node is $1$.  It makes sense that the second node has more impact on the dynamics as it has a strong feedback on the first node. 

Now we compute the four numerators that appear in $Q$
\eqa{
(\vec{w_1^\dag}\vec{w_1})  (\vec{v_1}\vec{v_1}\dag) &=& \left((1,3)\avec{1\\ 3}\right)  \left(\avec{1\\ 0}(1,0)\right)\\
   &=& (10) \cdot \avecc{1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0   } \\
(\vec{w_1^\dag}\vec{w_2})  (\vec{v_1}\vec{v_2^\dag}) &=& \left((1,3)\avec{0\\ 1}\right) \left(\avec{1 \\0 }(-3,1)\right) \\  
&=& (3) \cdot  \avecc{-3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0} \\  
(\vec{w_2^\dag}\vec{w_1})  (\vec{v_2}\vec{v_1^\dag}) &=& \left((0,1)\avec{1\\ 3}\right) \left(\avec{-3\\ 1}(1,0)\right) \\
   &=& (3) \cdot \avecc{-3 & 0 \\ 1 & 0}  \\
(\vec{w_2^\dag}\vec{w_2})  (\vec{v_2}\vec{v_2^\dag}) &=& \left((0,1)\avec{0 \\ 1}\right )  \left(\avec{-3\\ 1}(-3,1)\right) \\
  &=& (1) \cdot \avecc{9 & -3 \\ -3 & 1}
}
Hence the matrix is 
\eqa{
{\bf Q} &=& 5 \avecc{1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 } + \avecc{-3 & 1 \\ 0 & 0} + \avecc{-3 & 0 \\ 1 & 0}  + \frac{1}{4} \avecc{9 & -3 \\ -3 & 1} \\ 
   &=& \frac{1}{4}  \avecc{5 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 }
}
This shows that the first node with its controllability of $5/4$ is five times more sensitive to perturbations than the second node (controlability 1/4)
