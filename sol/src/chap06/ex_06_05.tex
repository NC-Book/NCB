
\exercise{Mean excess degree \label{exFriendsQ}}{2}
For the two networks from Ex.~6.1 (a) and (b) compute the excess degree distribution. Write the excess degree distribution such that $k$ appears only in the index of Kronecker deltas.

\solution
For the first of the two networks we already know from Ex.~6.1a, 
\eq{
p_k=\frac34 \delta_{k,4} + \frac14 \delta_{k,12},
}
and we know from Ex.~6.4,
\eq{
z=6.
}
We can now compute the excess degree distribution
\eqa{
q_k &=& (k+1)p_{k+1}/z \\
 &=& (k+1)\left( \frac34 \delta_{k+1,4} + \frac14 \delta_{k+1,12} \right)/6 \\
 &=& \frac{3(k+1)}{24} \delta_{k+1,4} + \frac{k+1}{24} \delta_{k+1,12}  \\
 &=& \frac{12}{24} \delta_{k+1,4} + \frac{12}{24} \delta_{k+1,12}  \\
 &=& \frac{1}{2} \delta_{k+1,4} + \frac{1}{2} \delta_{k+1,12}  \\
 &=& \frac{1}{2} \delta_{k,3} + \frac{1}{2} \delta_{k,11}.  
}
For the second network we know from Ex.~6.1b,
\eq{
p_k=\frac13 \delta_{k,3} + \frac13 \delta_{k,4} + \frac13 \delta_{k,14},
}
and from Ex.~6.4, 
\eq{
z=7.
}
We now compute the excess degree distribution 
\eqa{
q_k &=& (k+1)p_{k+1}/z \\
 &=& (k+1)\left(\frac13 \delta_{k+1,3} + \frac13 \delta_{k+1,4} + \frac13 \delta_{k+1,14}\right)/7 \\
 &=& \frac{k+1}{21} \delta_{k+1,3} + \frac{k+1}{21}\delta_{k+1,4} + \frac{k+1}{21} \delta_{k+1,14} \\
 &=& \frac3{21} \delta_{k+1,3} + \frac4{21}\delta_{k+1,4} + \frac{14}{21}\delta_{k+1,14} \\
 &=& \frac3{21} \delta_{k,2} + \frac4{21}\delta_{k,3} + \frac{14}{21}\delta_{k,13}.
 }
