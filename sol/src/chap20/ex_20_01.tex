\exercise{Multi-step adjacency \label{exMotifMulti}}{1}
Let $\lambda_n$ be the eigenvalues of $\bf A$. Show that the eigenvalues of ${\bf A}^k$ are ${\lambda_n}^k$. 

\solution 

We start from the eigenvector equation 
\eq{
  {\bf A} \vec{v_n} = \lambda_n \vec{v_n}.
}
Now we can show that 
\eqa{
   {\bf A}^k \vec{v_n} &=& {\bf A}^{k-1}{\bf A}  \vec{v_n}  \\ 
                       &=& {\bf A}^{k-1} \lambda_n \vec{v_n}  \\
                       &=& {\bf A}^{k-2} \lambda_n {\bf A} \vec{v_n}  \\
                       &=& {\bf A}^{k-2} {\lambda_n}^2 \vec{v_n}  \\
                       &=& {\bf A}^{k-m} {\lambda_n}^m \vec{v_n}  \\
                       &=& {\bf A}^{0} {\lambda_n}^k \vec{v_n}  \\
                       &=& {\lambda_n}^k \vec{v_n}  
}
In summary 
\eq{
({\bf A}^k) \vec{v_n} = ({\lambda_n}^k) \vec{v_n}
}
which shows that $\vec{v_n}$ is an eigenvector of ${\bf A}^k$ with eigenvalue ${\lambda_n}^k$.
