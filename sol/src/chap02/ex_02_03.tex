
\exercise{An abstract example}{2}
Find the route from the first to the fifth node in a network described by the distance matrix
\eq{
{\bf D} = \left( \begin{array}{c c c c c} 
0 & 3 & 6 & 8 & 9 \\
3 & 0 & 1 & 6 & 7 \\
6 & 1 & 0 & 3 & 1 \\
8 & 6 & 3 & 0 & 1 \\
9 & 7 & 1 & 1 & 0 \end{array}
\right).
}
(use Dijkstra, do not draw a map)

% solution %

\solution
Using Dijkstra's algorithm we find
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{c c c c c}
1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 \\\hline
0 & \oo & \oo & \oo & \oo \\
  & 3  & 6 & 8 & 9 \\
  &    & 4 & 8 & 9 \\
  &    &   & 7 & 5 \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
Hence, the shortest route is 1,2,3,5.
